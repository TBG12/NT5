Lock a Domain

You can lock a domain to prevent all users in the domain from connecting to the mail server and retrieving their email. When a domain is locked, the POP3 service still receives incoming e-mail for the domain and delivers it to the appropriate mailbox directory in the mail store. Outgoing mail from mailboxes in the domain is still sent through the Simple Mail Transfer Protocol (SMTP) service. Also, while a domain is locked, you can still perform administrative tasks such as deleting a domain.

To lock a domain

  1. On the primary navigation bar, click E-mail.
  2. Click Domains and Mailboxes.
  3. In the Name column, select the check box next to the domain you want to lock.
  4. Click Lock, and then click OK.

Notes

  • To perform this procedure, you must be a member of the Administrators group on the local computer, or you must have been delegated the appropriate authority. If the computer is joined to a domain, members of the Domain Admins group might be able to perform this procedure.
  • If you are using Active Directory integrated authentication, you must log on to the Active Directory domain, not the local computer, to perform this procedure.
  • While a domain is locked, you cannot unlock individual mailboxes. You must unlock the entire domain to unlock individual mailboxes.
